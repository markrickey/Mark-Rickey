Job Fair Data Analysis

Dynamic insights & interactive 3D visualization of employer registration and opportunity data from a public job fair event.

Data Analysis by Mark R. | Updated July 2025

Note: Some industry and opportunity fields were unstructured; trend and chart interpretations are simplified for clarity.

Key Takeaways

  • Manufacturing leads in demand: Highest employer participation and open positions.
  • Diverse opportunities: Not just full/part-time but also internships, apprenticeships, and seasonal roles.
  • Data improvement needed: Many "Unspecified" entries emphasize need for better structured data collection.

Detailed Analysis

Total Employers 43
Total Open Positions 1,473

Industry Leaders

  • Manufacturing: 15 employers, 97 positions (mostly full-time).
  • DoD: 1 employer, 25 positions (full & part-time, highlighting government roles).
  • Healthcare & Law Enforcement: Presence, but 0 reported positions—important for networking, not just hiring.
  • Other sectors: Transportation, Retail, Recruiters, and Other—smaller but important diversity contributors.

Opportunity Types Offered

  • Full-time: Most common type, present across industries.
  • Full-time & Part-time: Especially prevalent among Unspecified category—156 openings.
  • Internships & Apprenticeships: Indicate student and upskilling pathways.
  • Seasonal/Temp: Satisfy short-term local labor market needs.

Data Quality Challenge

The "Unspecified" industry makes up a large portion of records, underscoring a need for refined data entry in future fairs.
