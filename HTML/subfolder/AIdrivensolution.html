Roadmap to Freelance Technology Writing

A Strategic Guide to Leveraging Your Unique Expertise

Getting into Freelance Technology Writing is an excellent fit for your skills. It leverages your communication, research, and technical acumen, and offers the flexibility of remote work. The goal is to build a portfolio that showcases your writing ability and technical understanding, and then market yourself to relevant clients.

Phase 1: Build Your Portfolio

(The Foundation - Little to No Money)

Identify Your Niche(s)

Focus where your knowledge is deepest: Government/Public Sector Tech, Military Technology/Logistics, Data & Analytics, and Cybersecurity concepts. This makes you a specialist, which commands higher rates.

Create Writing Samples

You need 3-5 strong writing samples. Host these on your portfolio website in a "Writing Samples" or "Blog" section.

  • Repurpose Policy Memos: Adapt your Congressional briefs into articles (generalizing sensitive details).
  • Expand LinkedIn Posts: Turn your "Open to Work" stories into full-length blog posts.
  • Write About Your Projects: Create detailed "how-to" guides on your data management systems or optimization initiatives.
  • Explain Complex Concepts: Write a piece like "What is AI Alignment and Why It Matters for Public Service."

Phase 2: Market Yourself

(Finding Clients - Little to No Money)

Optimize LinkedIn

Update your headline and "About" section to include "Freelance Technology Writer." Feature your writing samples prominently.

Targeted Outreach

Identify and contact Content Managers at tech companies, government contractors, and consulting firms with a concise pitch and links to your samples.

Phase 3: Pricing & Payment

(How You Get Paid)

  • Per Word: Common for articles ($0.10 - $0.50+).
  • Per Project: A flat fee for a defined scope (e.g., $100-$500+ for a blog post).
  • Hourly: Possible for research-heavy or consulting projects.

Start by building those strong writing samples in your niche. Your unique background is a massive differentiator in the tech writing space.
